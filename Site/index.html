About

I work in UX testing, product support, QA, and training, focused on improving how users experience complex systems.

I specialize in identifying friction points in software, reproducing and documenting issues, and helping teams translate complex or unclear systems into something easier to understand and use. I enjoy hands-on testing, exploratory problem-solving, and improving documentation so both users and teams can work more effectively.

My background includes librarianship, technical writing, software development, project coordination, and customer support. Across these roles, I’ve consistently worked in “bridge” positions between systems and people—supporting users, clarifying workflows, and improving how information and tools are understood in practice.

I am particularly interested in roles involving UX testing, product support, QA (manual/exploratory), operations, and enablement work where I can help improve usability and reduce friction in real-world systems.
